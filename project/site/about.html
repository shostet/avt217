About

As an aspiring game designer, I see video games as platforms to showcase art and story, whatever that may mean to each player. Stories have always been a big part of my life, and I love the freedom that the media of games give designers to craft an interactive experience around a narrative, regardless of whether the game is story-driven or not.

Throughout my time at GMU so far, I’ve had many opportunities to experience team based game design. My classes have taught me the skills of working in groups, dealing with deadlines, and communicating while social-distancing. As the secretary of GADIG (Game Analysis and Design Interest Group) and GMU, these skills especially came in handy when it comes to organizing events, communicating announcements, and completing assets. Outside of Game Design, I also have plans to minor in Japanese Studies. Through language courses and self studying, my goal is to become fluent in Japanese.
