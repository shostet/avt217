Short Films

"Youth" Music Video

“Youth” was the first short film I made that made me fall in love with using visual storytelling. Through the cinematography and lyrics, I wanted to convey a sense of loss of innocence and relationships, particularly familial ones. Because of that, the film really drives home the rifts and faults that can occur between families as youths become adults. I played around with the idea of match cuts, a type of edit that uses an audio or visual cue to smoothly transition between two shots or scenes, often using juxtaposition. By using match cuts and color correction, I emphasized the passing of times with a cheerful past and a much darker future.

"The Days" Music Video

“The Days” played around with using found footage to convey nostalgia. Many of the clips, particularly the ones of my sister and me as children, were sent to me by my mom. Similarly to “Youth”, I really wanted to play around past and present storylines, and I think the lyrics of the song also emphasized nostalgia of good days. Unlike “Youth”, however, “The Days” paints a happier story of lost times.

"Days" Short Film

“Days” was an experimental art film using clips from my everyday life. Our assignment for our class was to take 10 short videos of anything during three different class periods, totalling in 30 clips. From there, we would piece together the clips to tell a story, whatever that might be. My daily routine was pretty consistent, so I used similar shots (being on the bus, car rides, walking around) to tie the entire piece together.
